Human-Computer Interaction & Computer Vision »

Kaixing ZHAO is a Ph.D. Candidate of Human-Computer Interaction at the Elipse team, Toulouse Institute of Computer Science Research (CNRS UMR 5505), which is a joint lab between CNRS (French National Center for Scientific Research) and University of Toulouse. His research is dedicated to designing novel interaction techniques in the field of ubiauitous computing, cognitive science and assistive technology. He is also interested in artificial intelligence technologies.

His supervisors are Dr. Christophe Jouffrais (Senior Research Scientist in Cognitive Science, CNRS, Director of IPAL Lab of CNRS & National Univerity of Singapore and Director of "Cherchons pour Voir" Lab) and Dr. Marcos Serrano (Associate Professor in Computer Science, University of Toulouse).
